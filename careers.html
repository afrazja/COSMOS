Your place in space

Here's a secret the movies don't tell you: you don't have to be an astronaut. Around 600 people have ever flown to space — but it took 400,000 people to put twelve boots on the Moon. Engineers, coders, doctors, artists. One of those seats has your name on it.

03 — From Your Bedroom to the Launch Pad

The path is real

Nobody is born a rocket engineer. Here's the actual route, starting tonight.

  1. Tonight

    Look up

    Find the Moon. Download a free star app and name one constellation. Curiosity is the fuel — everything else is engineering.

  2. This year

    Take your subjects seriously

    Math and science are the languages of space — and art, writing and biology have seats too. Ask your teacher one question you're afraid is silly. It isn't.

  3. Ages 10–18

    Build, join, compete

    Robotics club, astronomy club, coding games, science fairs, model rockets. Space agencies literally run competitions for students — someone has to win them.

  4. After school

    Train for your role

    Engineering, physics, medicine, computer science, design — pick the door that matches your card above. Scholarships for space-related study exist in almost every country.

  5. First job

    Get to the pad

    Internships at space agencies and companies are how almost everyone starts. The people flying rovers today were students asking questions ten years ago.
